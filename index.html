LAMIH

Research Assistant

keywords: AutoML, Computer Vision, Edge Computing, Transformers

LAMIH UMR CNRS (Laboratory of Automation, Mechanics and Industrial and Human Computing) is a joint research unit between the Polytechnic University of Hauts-de-France and the CNRS. The LAMIH has more than ever a preponderant place in the research that concerns the Human in engineering and systems with a recognized identity on its themes

Recently, Vision Transformers have enabled attention-based Deep Learning (DL) architectures to achieve remarkable results in Computer Vision (CV) tasks. However, due to the extensive computational resources required, these architectures are rarely implemented on resource-constrained platforms.

In our work, We, First, conducted a comparison study to analyze Vision Transformer's performance on several aspects : accuracy in detecting objects and Hardware efficiency measured by latency, memory consumption, and power consumption. Our study is done on different hardware platforms, including a CPU (intel i7), GPU (Nvidia Jetson Nano), and an embedded device (Raspberry Pi Model 3)

Based on the ideas and conclusions drawn from the comparative analysis, we developed HyT-NAS, an efficient Hardware-aware Neural Architecture Search (HW-NAS) including hybrid architectures targeting vision tasks on tiny devices. HyT-NAS improves state-of-the-art HW-NAS by enriching the search space, enhancing the search strategy as well as the performances predictors.

Our experiments show that HyT-NAS achieves a similar hypervolume with less than 5x training evaluations. Our resulting architecture outperforms MLPerf MobileNetV1 by 7% accuracy improvement with 10x less number of parameters on Person Recognition. The resulting paper (1st co-author) was presented in the Embedded System Week in october 2022.
